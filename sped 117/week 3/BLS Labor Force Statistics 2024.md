# Persons with a Disability: Labor Force Characteristics - 2024

Source: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (February 25, 2025)

## Key Statistics

- **Employment-population ratio (disabled):** 22.7% (series high since 2008)
- **Employment-population ratio (non-disabled):** 65.5%
- **Unemployment rate (disabled):** 7.5%
- **Unemployment rate (non-disabled):** 3.8%
- **Labor force participation rate (disabled):** 24.5% (series high)

| 2024 | 24.5% | 22.7% | 7.5% |

## Key Findings

- Half of all people with a disability were age 65 and over (nearly 3x the share for those without disability)
- Workers with a disability were nearly twice as likely to work part time
- Workers with a disability were more likely to be self-employed
- Unemployment rates were higher for people with disabilities across ALL educational attainment groups
- People who are White (13.0%) and Black (13.1%) had higher disability prevalence than Asian (6.8%) and Hispanic (8.7%)
